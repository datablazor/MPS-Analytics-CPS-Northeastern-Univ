    Given the three tables shown below: patient_fact, cpt_dim, and patient_product_dim, write an SQL Query that will give the following output as a result:

    product_line cpt_description sum_total_paid
    COMMERCIAL NEW PATIENT VISIT LEVEL 2 300
    COMMERCIAL ESTABLISHED PATIENT VISIT LEVEL 3 350

     

    Tables:

    patient_fact:

    PATIENT_ID DATE_OF_SERVICE CPT_CODE TOTAL_PAID
    P0001 1/6/2015 99202 300
    P0001 1/12/2015 99213 350
    P0002 2/20/2015 99212 250
    P0002 2/25/2015 99212 250
    P0002 1/6/2015 90658 100
    P0003 1/6/2015 99214 450

    cpt_dim:

    CPT_CODE CPT_DESCRIPTION
    90658 INFLUENZA VACCINE
    99202 NEW PATIENT VISIT LEVEL 2
    99212 ESTABLISHED PATIENT VISIT LEVEL 2
    99213 ESTABLISHED PATIENT VISIT LEVEL 3
    99214 ESTABLISHED PATIENT VISIT LEVEL 4

    patient_product_dim:

    PATIENT_ID PRODUCT_LINE
    P0001 COMMERCIAL
    P0002 MEDICARE
    P0003 MEDICARE 
    Your Answer:

    select pd.PRODUCT_LINE,  cpt.CPT_DESCRIPTION, SUM(pf.TOTAL_PAID)

    from patient_fact pf

    inner join cpt_dim cpt 

    on pf.CPT_CODE = cf.CPT_CODE

    inner join patient_product_dim pd

    on pf.PATIENT_ID = pd.PATIENT_ID

    group by pd.PRODUCT_LINE;

    select c.product_line,b.cpt_description,sum(a.total_paid) as sum_total_paid from patient_fact a,cpt_dim b,patient_product_dim c where a.cpt_code = b.cpt_code and a.patient_id = c.patient_id and c.product_line = 'COMMERCIAL' group by c.product_line,b.cpt_description;

    What is the difference between a one-to-one join and a one-to-many join? Give one example of each kind of join.
    Your Answer:

    One-to-one relationships associate one record in one table with a single record in the other table. One-to-many relationships associate one record in one table with many records in the other table.

    In terms of table structure, one-to-one can have a bidirectional relationship between its tables foreign keys(i.e foreign key of Table A in the attribute list of table B and vice-versa) which is not possible in a one-to-many relationship.

    Explain the difference in processing between a star-schema and an OLAP Cube. What are the benefits and drawbacks of each type?
    Your Answer:

    Star schemas are dimensional structures deployed in a relational database management system (RDBMS). They characteristically consist of fact tables linked to associated dimension tables via primary/foreign key relationships. An online analytical processing (OLAP) cube is a dimensional structure implemented in a multidimensional database; it can be equivalent in content to, or more often derived from, a relational star schema. An OLAP cube contains dimensional attributes and facts, but it is accessed through languages with more analytic capabilities than SQL, such as XMLA. OLAP cubes are included in this list of basic techniques because an OLAP cube is often the final step in the deployment of a dimensional DW/BI system, or may exist as an aggregate structure based on a more atomic relational star schema.
